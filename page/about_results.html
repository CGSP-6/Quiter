Our Findings

Our findings showed that by using commute-based method, the level of noise pollution for walking routine can be measured. Other than that, this project proves that people can reduce the noise pollution exposure by include green spaces in their walking routine as the noise level in green spaces is much lower than the outside.

The results from this project demonstrates that if pedestrians choose the routes in green spaces to reach their destination, they will be less exposed to noise. Although, some of the paths which include green spaces takes longer time and route but the option will give different impact and play a remarkable role in reducing the exposure of noise pollution. This can reduce the negative health impact such as hypertension and stress.

The Walking Scenarios in Edinburgh

There are four scenarios of walking routine by using the routes inside of four different greenspaces that had been created in this project which are Meadows Park, Princes Street Garden, Bruntsfield Link Park and Braidburn Valley Park. The route in the parks were proved to have a low noise level because the route are far from the traffic congestion.

Scenario 3: A student walking from shop to the Scottish National Gallery by using the route in Princes Street Garden.

Scenario 4: A student walking from his accommodation to school by using the route in Meadows Park.

Link Greenspaces Accessibility with the SIMD

SIMD is stand for Scottish Index of Multiple Deprivation. This index measure the deprivation in Scotland area in term of resources and income. Deprived income area illustrate that the people in that area has low income (Scottish Government, 2020). More information can be access here in the website of Scottish Government.

The quiet green space area of each data zone were measured and categorised. The quiet green space area of Edinburgh is below. The value 1 represents maximum 10% of the green space in that area while value 10 represents less than 10% of the green space in the area (Map 1). People who lives in the areas that have green spaces at the same time income deprived and choose walking for commute, we provide suggestions walking route for their travel. We encourage people to use the green spaces to escape from noise pollution. The areas with black line border are low income with 10% green spaces accessibility (Map 2).This is one of the suggestion for the park, the purple route is the route that is quieter and easier to pass in the generated recommended green space (Map 3). As the analysis was performed on all roads and green spaces in Edinburgh, paths were also generated in other green spaces, some of which substantially overlapped existing paths, demonstrating the generality and practicality of our design. These are the examples of walking route using Meadows Park (Map 4).
